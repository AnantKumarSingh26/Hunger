About Zero Hunger

Zero Hunger, a key Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) by the United Nations, strives to eradicate hunger, ensure food security, enhance nutrition, and foster sustainable agriculture by 2030. It addresses global challenges like poverty, inequality, and climate change, aiming to create a world where everyone has access to sufficient, safe, and nutritious food.

Hunger is a multifaceted issue that extends beyond the mere absence of food. It is deeply intertwined with systemic challenges such as poverty, inequality, and the impacts of climate change. Poverty limits access to resources, inequality creates disparities in food distribution, and climate change disrupts agricultural productivity. Addressing these root causes is essential to achieving sustainable solutions.

Goals & Objectives

Food Security

Ensure access to safe, nutritious, and sufficient food for all people year-round.

Sustainable Agriculture

Promote sustainable farming practices to increase productivity and resilience.

Improved Nutrition

End all forms of malnutrition, especially among children and vulnerable groups.

Plans & Strategies

  • Invest in small-scale farmers and rural development.
  • Promote climate-resilient agricultural practices.
  • Reduce food waste and improve distribution systems.
  • Strengthen social protection systems for vulnerable populations.
  • Encourage global partnerships and funding for hunger eradication.
